About

I’ve spent years doing work that doesn’t grab attention until something breaks. I maintain websites after launch, manage digital systems once the initial excitement fades, and repair technical issues as they arise. In my current role, I support a remote organization by keeping its digital infrastructure stable and usable—maintaining the website, improving workflows, automating repetitive tasks, and ensuring the tools people rely on continue to work month after month. I’m often the person quietly ensuring things don’t go wrong.

Before this, I worked in public libraries and technology companies, helping people find information, navigate systems, and solve practical problems in real time. I’ve worked with clients, patrons, and teams who needed clarity more than polish. Outside of the office, I’ve done archaeological fieldwork in Saskatchewan—slow, methodical work that rewards patience and attention over speed.

Through all of this, I’ve learned that I excel in steady ownership: showing up consistently, understanding how systems age, and improving them thoughtfully over time. I’m not interested in theatrics. I’m interested in work that lasts.
