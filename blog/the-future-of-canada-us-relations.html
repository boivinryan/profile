The Future of Canada–U.S. Relations

February 2026 · International Trade

Canada’s trade relationship with the United States is among the deepest and most integrated in the world. In 2024, roughly three-quarters of Canada’s domestic exports were destined for the U.S., making it by far Canada’s largest trading partner.

This proximity and volume confer real advantages: efficient transport links, shared regulatory frameworks under the USMCA, and highly integrated supply chains. But heavy reliance on a single partner also represents a structural risk.

The size of the United States market makes it indispensable, but Canada’s economic security requires engagement with multiple centers of demand.

Diversifying export markets has therefore emerged as a central policy aim. The European Union is Canada’s second-largest trading partner and represents a substantial, rules-based bloc for Canadian goods and services.

At the same time, China remains an important but complex partner. While opportunities exist, geopolitical and regulatory frictions persist, requiring selective and managed engagement.

The strategic imperative is not to reduce the U.S. role, but to increase Canada’s economic resilience by building multiple, durable links in the global economy.
